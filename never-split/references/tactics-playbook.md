## Tactic Quick Reference

### Mirroring (미러링)

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Repeat 1-3 key words | Repeat entire sentences |
| Use curious upward inflection | Use mocking or flat tone |
| Follow with 4+ seconds of silence | Rush to fill the silence |
| Mirror when confused or want more info | Mirror every single sentence |

**Starter phrases:**
- "I'm sorry, [mirror]...?"
- "[Mirror]?"
- "What do you mean by [mirror]?"

---

### Labeling (라벨링)

**Formula:** "It {seems/sounds/looks/feels} like..." + emotion/situation

**Positive emotions (reinforce 강화):**
- "It seems like you really care about quality."
- "It sounds like your team has worked incredibly hard on this."
- "It looks like fairness is a core value for you."

**Negative emotions (diminish 완화):**
- "It seems like you're frustrated with the timeline."
- "It sounds like there's a lot of pressure coming from above."
- "It looks like you're worried this won't work."

**After labeling:** STOP. WAIT. COUNT TO 5 IN YOUR HEAD.

Common responses to labels:
- "That's right" → Breakthrough. Keep going.
- "That's right, and also..." → Even better. They're opening up.
- "No, that's not it" → Fine. Say "I didn't mean to imply that. Help me understand." They'll clarify.
- Silence → Let them process. Don't rescue.

---

### Calibrated Questions (보정 질문)

**The Big 5:**

| Question | When to Use |
|---|---|
| "How am I supposed to do that?" | When their ask is unreasonable |
| "What about this is important to you?" | To discover underlying interests |
| "How can we solve this problem?" | To make them a partner |
| "What are we trying to accomplish?" | To reset a derailed conversation |
| "How will we know we're on track?" | To test commitment (Rule of Three) |

**More calibrated questions by situation:**

**When they push back:**
- "What would you need to make this work?"
- "How does this fit with your priorities?"
- "What's the biggest challenge you see?"

**When they go silent:**
- "What's holding you back?"
- "What would it take for you to move forward?"
- "What concerns do you have that we haven't addressed?"

**When exploring their world:**
- "What does a successful outcome look like for you?"
- "How does this affect your team?"
- "What happens if we don't resolve this?"

**When closing:**
- "How do you want to proceed?"
- "What's the next step from your perspective?"
- "How can I make this easier for you?"

### Accusation Audit (비난 감사)

**Template for common scenarios:**

**Asking for a raise:**
"I know this might seem like bad timing. You probably think I'm not seeing the big picture of what the company is going through. You might feel like I'm being ungrateful for the opportunities I've already been given..."

**Delivering bad news to a client:**
"You're probably going to think we dropped the ball. You might feel like we haven't been taking your project seriously. This is going to sound like we're making excuses..."

**Renegotiating a contract:**
"You're probably going to think we're trying to take advantage of the situation. It might seem like we're not honoring our word. You might feel like this is a bait-and-switch..."

**Key:** Always do the accusation audit BEFORE stating your case. Let the accusations land, then pivot.

---

### Getting to "That's Right" (바로 그거예요)

**The formula:** Paraphrase + Label = "That's Right"

**Step-by-step:**
1. Listen actively to everything they say
2. Paraphrase the MEANING (not just the words): "So what you're saying is..."
3. Label the EMOTION underneath: "...and it sounds like that's been really frustrating."
4. Deliver both together
5. Wait for "That's right"

**If you get "You're right" instead:**
- They're dismissing you politely
- Go back to labeling: "It seems like I'm missing something."
- Ask: "What am I getting wrong?"
- Try again with a better summary

### Ackerman Model (에커먼 모델) — Step by Step

**Setup:**
1. Determine your target price (목표 가격)
2. Calculate: 65%, 85%, 95%, 100% of target
3. Prepare a non-monetary item for the final round

**Execution:**

| Round | % of Target | Script |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 65% | "Based on [research/comparables], I was thinking [65%]." Use empathy. "I know this might seem low..." |
| — | — | *Label their reaction. Mirror. Let them counter.* |
| 2 | 85% | "I want to make this work. [85%] is really stretching for me." Show genuine reluctance. |
| — | — | *Calibrated question: "How am I supposed to make that work?"* |
| 3 | 95% | "I really have been going back and forth on this. [95%]. I don't know if I can go any further." Decreasing increments signal your limit. |
| — | — | *Label: "It seems like we're very close."* |
| 4 | 100% | "[Precise number, e.g., 49,850]. That's my absolute number. I can also [non-monetary item]." |

**Non-monetary sweeteners (비금전 양보):**
- Extended warranty
- Free delivery/installation
- Training sessions
- Maintenance period
- Priority support
- Flexible payment terms

## Dealing with Difficult Counterparts

### The Assertive Type (주장형)
- They want to be heard. Let them talk first.
- Mirror and label heavily.
- Use calibrated questions to redirect.
- They respect directness — don't be meek.

### The Analyst Type (분석형)
- They want data and time to think.
- Give them detailed information.
- Don't rush them.
- Use precise numbers and logic alongside empathy.

### The Accommodator Type (순응형)
- They want the relationship.
- Build rapport before business.
- Watch for counterfeit "yes" — they hate saying no.
- Use the Rule of Three to verify commitment.

## Email & Text Negotiation

**Subject line:** Use their name or a provocative question
**Rule:** One screenful maximum. Don't give them a novel.

**If they've gone silent:**
"Have you given up on [the project/this partnership/finding a solution]?"
→ This no-oriented question almost always gets a response.

**The Late Night FM DJ Voice (심야 라디오 DJ 목소리):**
Even in writing, use a calm, measured, warm tone. No exclamation marks. No ALL CAPS. No urgency. Slow is smooth, smooth is fast.

---

## Voss's Three Voice Types

| Voice | When | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Late Night DJ** (기본 음성) | Default — use 80% of the time | Calm, slow, downward inflection. Soothing. |
| **Assertive / Direct** | Rarely — only to set a clear boundary | Declarative. Short. Matter-of-fact. |
| **Playful / Positive** | Building rapport, softening tough asks | Light, encouraging. Smile while you talk. |

## Common Mistakes Checklist

Before any negotiation, review:

- [ ] Did I prepare an accusation audit?
- [ ] Do I have 5+ calibrated questions ready?
- [ ] Am I prepared to hear "no" and use it?
- [ ] Do I know my walk-away point?
- [ ] Am I using "It seems like" not "I think"?
- [ ] Am I avoiding "Why" questions?
- [ ] Do I have my Ackerman numbers calculated?
- [ ] Am I planning to listen more than talk?
- [ ] Do I know what Black Swans I'm hunting for?
- [ ] Am I committed to NOT splitting the difference?
