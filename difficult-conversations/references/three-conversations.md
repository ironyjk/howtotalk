# Three Conversations — Detailed Reference (세 가지 대화 상세 가이드)

## Quick Diagnostic (빠른 진단)

When stuck in a difficult conversation, ask: "Which conversation am I actually in?"

| Signal | Likely Conversation | Action |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Arguing facts, debating "what really happened" | What Happened | Shift to contribution mapping |
| Feeling tense, resentful, or shut down | Feelings | Name and share the emotion |
| Feeling defensive, anxious, or "this defines me" | Identity | Ground yourself in complexity |

---

## Conversation 1 Deep Dive: "What Happened?" (사실 대화 심층)

### The Contribution System

Instead of: "Whose fault is it?" → Ask: "What's the system that produced this?"

**Example — Missed project deadline**:

| Person | Contribution |
|--------|-------------|
| Manager | Changed scope mid-sprint without updating timeline |
| Developer | Didn't flag the risk when scope changed |
| PM | Didn't update the Gantt chart |
| Organization | No formal change-request process |

Notice: When you map contributions, the "villain" disappears. It's a system failure.

### Intent vs Impact (의도 vs 영향)

```
Their Intent    →    [BLACK BOX]    →    Your Impact
(unknown to you)   (communication)    (known to you)

Your Intent     →    [BLACK BOX]    →    Their Impact
(known to you)     (communication)    (unknown to you)
```

Key insight: You know YOUR intent and THEIR impact. You do NOT know their intent or your impact. This asymmetry causes most conflicts.

**Instead of**: "Why did you do that to me?"
**Try**: "When X happened, the impact on me was Y. I'm curious what was going on for you."

## Conversation 2 Deep Dive: The Feelings Conversation (감정 대화 심층)

### The Emotional Footprint (감정 발자국)

Every person has an emotional footprint — patterns from childhood/experience that amplify certain triggers:

- **Anxious footprint**: Need for certainty, control → triggered by ambiguity
- **Shame footprint**: Need for approval → triggered by criticism
- **Anger footprint**: Need for justice/fairness → triggered by perceived unfairness
- **Withdrawal footprint**: Need for safety → triggered by conflict itself

Knowing your footprint helps you distinguish between:
- Feelings caused by THIS situation (appropriate)
- Feelings amplified by YOUR footprint (disproportionate)

### Expressing Feelings Without Blame (비난 없이 감정 표현)

| Blaming | Non-blaming |
|---------|-------------|
| "You made me angry" | "I felt angry when that happened" |
| "You don't respect me" | "I didn't feel respected in that moment" |
| "You're being unfair" | "This feels unfair to me, and I want to understand why" |

### Acknowledging Their Feelings (상대 감정 인정)

Phrases that work:
- "It sounds like this has been really frustrating for you." (정말 답답했겠군요)
- "I can see why you'd feel that way given how you experienced it." (그렇게 느끼실 수 있겠네요)
- "That sounds hard." (힘드셨겠네요)

Phrases that backfire:
- "I understand exactly how you feel." (presumes too much)
- "You shouldn't feel that way." (invalidating)
- "Calm down." (dismissive)

## Conversation 3 Deep Dive: The Identity Conversation (정체성 대화 심층)

### The Three Core Questions (세 가지 핵심 질문)

| Question | Threat | Reframe |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Am I competent? (유능한가?) | "This mistake proves I'm incompetent" | "I made a mistake AND I am generally competent" |
| Am I a good person? (좋은 사람인가?) | "This conflict means I'm a bad person" | "I contributed to this AND I have good intentions" |
| Am I lovable? (사랑받을 자격이 있는가?) | "If they see the real me, they'll reject me" | "I can be imperfect AND still be valued" |

### Identity Quake Scale (정체성 지진 규모)

Rate the threat level before entering the conversation:

- **1-3**: Minor tremor. Standard disagreement. (경미한 진동)
- **4-6**: Moderate shake. One core question is activated. (중간 흔들림)
- **7-9**: Major quake. Multiple core questions threatened. Prepare carefully. (큰 지진)
- **10**: Existential. Identity crisis territory. Consider professional support. (존재적 위기)

### Grounding Exercise (안정화 연습)

Before the conversation:
1. Write down: "The worst thing they could say to me is: ___"
2. Write down: "If that were partly true, it would mean: ___"
3. Write down: "AND, it is also true that: ___"
4. Accept the complexity. You contain multitudes. (복잡성을 받아들여라)

## Third Story Templates (제3의 이야기 템플릿)

### For Workplace Conflicts
> "I think we may have different expectations about [topic], and I'd like to understand where we each are coming from so we can figure this out together."

### For Relationship Conflicts
> "I've noticed some tension between us around [topic]. I have my own perspective, but I realize I might be missing yours. Could we talk about it?"

### For Giving Bad News
> "I have something difficult to share. Before I do, I want you to know that I've thought about how to say this in a way that's fair to both of us."

### For Receiving Complaints
> "I hear that you're frustrated about [topic]. I want to understand what's happening from your side before I share my perspective."

## Decision Tree: Should I Have This Conversation? (대화를 해야 할까?)

```
Is this about my feelings or the situation?
├── Mostly my feelings → Journal first, then decide
└── The situation needs to change →
    ├── Have I examined my contribution? (yes/no)
    │   └── No → Do that first
    └── Yes →
        ├── Is there a simpler way? (email, quick clarification)
        │   └── Yes → Try that first
        └── No → Prepare using the Three Conversations framework
```
