# Radical Candor Feedback Guide (래디컬 캔더 피드백 가이드)

## SBI Framework — Detailed Reference

### Situation (상황)

Set the context. Be specific about time, place, and circumstance.

**Good**: "During today's standup at 9:15am..."
**Bad**: "Lately in meetings..."

Rules:
- Use observable facts, not interpretations
- Recent events only (within 48 hours)
- One situation per feedback session

### Behavior (행동)

Describe what the person **did**, not what you think they **are**.

**Good**: "You spoke over the QA lead twice while she was presenting her findings."
**Bad**: "You were being disrespectful."

Rules:
- Use action verbs (said, did, wrote, sent)
- Avoid adjectives about character (lazy, careless, rude)
- Be specific enough that a camera could have captured it

### Impact (영향)

Explain the effect — on you, the team, the project, or the business.

**Good**: "The QA lead looked deflated and didn't share her remaining 3 findings. We may have missed critical bugs."
**Bad**: "It made things awkward."

Three levels of impact:
1. **Personal**: "I felt blindsided when..."
2. **Team**: "The team lost confidence in..."
3. **Business**: "The client noticed and..."

## Praise Examples (칭찬 예시)

### Ruinous Empathy (bad)
> "Great presentation today!"

### Radical Candor (good)
> "In the board meeting, when you walked them through the unit economics slide, you anticipated their objection about CAC and addressed it before they could ask. That's why they approved the budget increase. Your preparation showed."

---

## Criticism Examples (비판 예시)

### Obnoxious Aggression (bad)
> "Your code is garbage. Did you even test it?"

### Manipulative Insincerity (bad)
> "Oh, it's fine, don't worry about it..." (then complains to others)

### Ruinous Empathy (bad)
> (Says nothing because they don't want to hurt feelings)

### Radical Candor (good)
> "Hey, I wanted to talk about the deployment yesterday. The service went down for 40 minutes because the migration script wasn't tested against staging data. I know you were under time pressure, but skipping staging tests creates real risk. Can we figure out a process that works even when you're rushed?"

## The HHIPP Check (before giving feedback)

Before you speak, verify:

- **H**umble: Am I open to being wrong?
- **H**elpful: Is this for their benefit, not my venting?
- **I**mmediate: Am I giving this promptly?
- **P**rivate: Am I delivering criticism in private? (praise can be public)
- **P**ersonalized: Does this person prefer direct or softer delivery?

## Common Quadrant Traps by Role

| Role | Common Trap | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| New Manager | Ruinous Empathy | Wants to be liked |
| Senior Engineer | Obnoxious Aggression | Values correctness over relationships |
| HR/People Ops | Manipulative Insincerity | Trained to be "diplomatic" |
| CEO/Founder | Oscillates OA ↔ RE | Cares deeply but lacks calibration |

## Receiving Feedback: The AAAA Protocol

1. **Absorb**: Listen without defending. Let the words land.
2. **Acknowledge**: "Thank you for telling me. That took courage."
3. **Ask**: "Can you give me a specific example?" / "What would better look like?"
4. **Act**: Within 1 week, demonstrate change. Close the loop: "I tried X differently because of your feedback."

## Manager's Weekly Cadence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Monday | Observe 2-3 interactions intentionally |
| Tuesday-Thursday | Deliver at least 1 piece of Radical Candor feedback |
| Friday | Reflect: Did I slip into another quadrant this week? |
