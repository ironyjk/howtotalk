# Framework Comparison & Selection Guide

## The 13 Frameworks — Quick Reference

| # | Framework | Core Idea | Best For |
|---|-----------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | **NVC** | Observation-Feeling-Need-Request | Expressing without blame |
| 2 | **Radical Candor** | Care Personally + Challenge Directly | Feedback and management |
| 3 | **Crucial Conversations** | Make it safe to talk about anything | High-stakes disagreements |
| 4 | **Difficult Conversations** | Three Conversations (What Happened, Feelings, Identity) | Emotionally loaded topics |
| 5 | **Never Split the Difference** | Tactical empathy + calibrated questions | Negotiations and hostage-style standoffs |
| 6 | **Getting to Yes** | Interests, not positions; BATNA | Principled negotiation |
| 7 | **Influence** | 6 principles of persuasion | Ethical persuasion and marketing |
| 8 | **Storytelling** | Narrative structure for persuasion | Presentations, pitches, leadership |
| 9 | **Motivational Interviewing** | Roll with resistance, elicit change talk | Helping people change |
| 10 | **Socratic Method** | Questions that lead to insight | Teaching, coaching, workshops |
| 11 | **SCARF** | 5 social threat/reward domains | Team dynamics, change management |
| 12 | **Active Listening** | Empathic presence + reflection techniques | Any conversation, especially emotional |
| 13 | **DESC** | Describe-Express-Specify-Consequences | Assertive boundary-setting |

## Decision Flowchart

```
START
  |
  v
Is someone in immediate danger or crisis?
  YES --> Crisis response (not a communication framework problem)
  NO  --> Continue
  |
  v
What is the primary goal?
  |
  +--> UNDERSTAND someone --> Active Listening
  +--> EXPRESS your needs --> NVC or DESC
  +--> PERSUADE someone --> Influence + Storytelling
  +--> NEGOTIATE an agreement --> Getting to Yes + Never Split
  +--> GIVE FEEDBACK --> SCARF + Radical Candor
  +--> RESOLVE CONFLICT --> Crucial Conversations + NVC
  +--> HAVE A HARD TALK --> Difficult Conversations + Active Listening
  +--> MOTIVATE CHANGE --> Motivational Interviewing + Socratic
  +--> SET BOUNDARIES --> DESC + Radical Candor
  +--> BUILD RAPPORT --> Active Listening + SCARF
  +--> TEACH/COACH --> Socratic + Storytelling
  +--> NOT SURE --> Universal Fallback Sequence
```

## When to Use What — Decision Table

| You Need To... | First Choice | Why | Second Choice |
|----------------|-------------|-----|---------------|
| Give negative feedback to someone you manage | Radical Candor | Direct + caring = growth | SCARF (pre-diagnose threats) |
| Negotiate salary or contract | Never Split the Difference | Tactical empathy wins deals | Getting to Yes (principled backup) |
| Express hurt to someone you love | NVC | Non-blame expression of needs | Difficult Conversations |
| Run a meeting where opinions clash | Crucial Conversations | Safety framework prevents shutdown | Active Listening |
| Convince a boardroom | Storytelling | Narrative moves groups | Influence (structural persuasion) |
| Help someone see they need to change | Motivational Interviewing | Self-motivation > external pressure | Socratic (question-led insight) |
| Understand why a team member is disengaged | SCARF | Map threat domains | Active Listening |
| Sell a product/service | Influence | 6 principles designed for this | Storytelling |
| Get someone to actually hear you | Active Listening (first!) | You must listen before asking to be heard | NVC |
| Set a clear boundary | DESC | 4-step assertive script | Radical Candor |
| Have a conversation you've been avoiding | Difficult Conversations | Designed for exactly this | Crucial Conversations |
| Lead a training session | Socratic | Questions > lectures | Storytelling |
| Navigate cross-cultural tension | SCARF | Universal threat/reward model | NVC (universal needs) |
| Apologize authentically | Difficult Conversations | Three Conversations framework | NVC |
| Mediate between two people | Getting to Yes | Interests-based resolution | Crucial Conversations |

## Framework Strengths & Weaknesses

| Framework | Strength | Weakness | Watch Out For |
|-----------|----------|----------|---------------|
| NVC | Non-blame expression | Can sound formulaic | "Robot NVC" — mechanical OFNR feels fake |
| Radical Candor | Clarity + caring | Misused as license to be blunt | "Obnoxious Aggression" quadrant |
| Crucial Conversations | Safety management | Complex (many tools) | Over-focusing on safety, avoiding the point |
| Difficult Conversations | Deep preparation | Slow, not for quick interactions | Analysis paralysis before the conversation |
| Never Split | Tactical power | Can feel manipulative | Using tactics without genuine empathy |
| Getting to Yes | Fair, principled | Assumes good faith from both sides | Doesn't work with truly bad-faith actors |
| Influence | Proven principles | Ethical boundary is thin | Manipulation vs. ethical influence |
| Storytelling | Emotionally compelling | Weak on logic and specifics | All story, no substance |
| Motivational Interviewing | Respects autonomy | Requires patience | Frustrating when YOU have urgency |
| Socratic | Deepens thinking | Feels interrogative if overdone | "Socratic bulldozing" — leading to YOUR conclusion |
| SCARF | Neurological insight | Diagnostic, not conversational | Over-analyzing instead of just talking |
| Active Listening | Universal foundation | Not sufficient alone for complex issues | Endless listening without ever expressing |
| DESC | Clear and actionable | Can feel rigid or scripted | Mechanical delivery kills the message |

## Emotion Level x Framework Matrix

| Emotion Level | Use These | Avoid These | Why |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Crisis (9-10) | Active Listening | Everything else | Brain is hijacked; only calm presence works |
| High (7-8) | Active Listening, SCARF, NVC (feelings only) | DESC, Radical Candor, Influence | Logic won't land; validate first |
| Medium (4-6) | ALL frameworks available | None excluded | The productive zone for structured approaches |
| Low (1-3) | DESC, Getting to Yes, Radical Candor | NVC feelings, Active Listening reflection | May feel patronizing when emotion is low |

## Power Dynamic x Framework Matrix

| Power Dynamic | Recommended | Caution | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|---------|-------|
| You're senior | Radical Candor, Socratic | DESC (feels dictatorial), Never Split (feels manipulative) | Your words carry extra weight; soften |
| You're junior | DESC, Getting to Yes | Radical Candor (can backfire with insecure boss) | Frame as "I" not "you"; manage their Status domain |
| Equal peers | NVC, Crucial Conversations | Influence (can feel like politicking) | Direct and mutual works best |
| Customer/vendor | Getting to Yes, Never Split | NVC (too personal for transactions) | Keep it professional but human |
| Intimate partner | NVC, Difficult Conversations | DESC (too formal), Influence (manipulative) | Vulnerability > structure |
| Stranger | Active Listening, Influence | NVC (too intimate), Radical Candor (no relationship yet) | Build rapport first |

## Pipeline Quick-Select

For common situations, use these pre-built pipelines:

```
FEEDBACK       = SCARF → Radical Candor → Active Listening
NEGOTIATION    = Getting to Yes → Never Split → Influence
HARD TALK      = Difficult Conversations → NVC → Active Listening
PERSUASION     = Influence → Storytelling → Socratic
TEAM CONFLICT  = Crucial Conversations → NVC → Getting to Yes
CHANGE MGMT    = SCARF → Motivational Interviewing → Socratic
BOUNDARIES     = Active Listening → DESC → NVC
TEACHING       = Socratic → Storytelling → Active Listening
APOLOGY        = Difficult Conversations → NVC → Active Listening
MEDIATION      = Crucial Conversations → Active Listening → Getting to Yes
```

## Anti-Patterns

### The Framework Collector
Knows all 13 frameworks but uses them as intellectual shields to avoid being vulnerable. Frameworks are tools, not armor.

### The Technique Robot
Applies techniques mechanically. "I notice you said X (mirroring). That sounds frustrating (labeling)." People feel processed, not heard.

### The Framework Hammer
Has a favorite framework and uses it for everything. "When all you have is NVC, everything looks like an unmet need."

### The Analyzer
Spends so much time diagnosing the situation (which domain? which framework? which pipeline?) that they never actually have the conversation.

### The Switcher
Changes frameworks mid-sentence when the current one isn't "working." Comes across as erratic and inauthentic.

**The Cure for All Anti-Patterns:** Genuine care for the other person. If your intent is right, imperfect technique works. If your intent is wrong, perfect technique fails.
